01 — THE PROJECT

Beyond the Rankings

This project analyzes the Top 10 LET passers in the Philippines from 2018 to 2023 to identify patterns in institutional performance, regional representation, demographics, and examination ratings.

Rather than treating board examination rankings as isolated results, the study applies descriptive analytics to transform ranking records into measurable trends that can support evidence-based comparisons across universities and regions.

THE BOTTOM LINE

Educational quality should be measured through evidence—not geography or reputation alone.

The LET Top 10 data demonstrates that high-performing candidates and institutions emerge across regions, with Mindanao showing particularly strong representation during the analyzed period. Rankings provide one useful signal, but stronger conclusions require combining them with broader indicators of educational quality.

CONTEXT NOTE

Looking Beyond the Rankings

Philippine educational prestige has historically been perceived as concentrated around Metro Manila and Luzon, where many prominent universities, recognized academic programs, and institutional opportunities are located.

Research also points to geographic inequalities in access to educational resources. Studies by the Philippine Institute for Development Studies (PIDS) have documented uneven regional patterns in public education spending, school infrastructure, access to higher education, and the distribution of recognized Centers of Excellence and Centers of Development.

Against this broader context, the strong representation of Mindanao-based universities among LET Top 10 performers is particularly noteworthy. It suggests that strong academic outcomes are not exclusive to institutions located in the country's traditional centers of educational prestige.

HISTORICAL
Uneven Education Funding

Earlier PIDS analysis found substantial regional differences in education subsidies. Southern Luzon received the largest share of elementary education subsidy in the period studied, while regions such as ARMM received much smaller shares in some levels of education.

2025
Concentration of Recognized Programs

PIDS research on teacher education found that Luzon, particularly NCR, stands out for its concentration of Centers of Excellence and Centers of Development, while funding and professional-development constraints remain concerns among weaker-performing institutions.

2024
Geographic Gaps in Higher Education

Research on public higher-education expansion identified concentrations in NCR, CALABARZON, and Central Luzon, while underserved areas—particularly parts of Mindanao— continue to face barriers to access.

Interpretation: These findings do not prove that Mindanao has a better education system overall, nor that every Luzon region receives more funding. Rather, they provide context for understanding why Mindanao's strong LET performance is significant despite persistent regional differences in educational resources and opportunities.
