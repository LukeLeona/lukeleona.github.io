COMMUNITY • VOLUNTEERISM • PROFESSIONAL GROWTH

From Student Volunteer to Marketing Co-Lead

How Python Philippines helped transform me from a quiet student who hesitated to share ideas into someone trusted to lead, communicate, collaborate, and help shape community initiatives.

WRITTEN BY Luke Leona
PUBLISHED 18 June 2025
READ TIME 6 min read
KEY IDEA

Sometimes the experience that changes your career most is not found in a classroom or job description. It begins simply by showing up, contributing, and finding the courage to use your voice.

I originally joined Python Philippines because I wanted to learn, contribute, and become closer to the technology community. I did not expect volunteering to change how I communicate, lead, and see myself as a professional.

At the beginning, I was curious but quiet. I could think of ideas, observe problems, and imagine solutions, yet I often hesitated to speak because I was unsure whether my contribution was good enough.

“
Confidence did not arrive before I participated. It grew because I participated.
— A lesson from my volunteer journey
01
THE BEGINNING

Curious Enough to Join. Not Yet Confident Enough to Speak.

I joined Python Philippines as a student volunteer driven mainly by curiosity. I wanted to learn more about Python, meet people in technology, and understand how communities organize events and initiatives.

But during meetings, I often stayed quiet. I had ideas and opinions, yet self-doubt made me question whether they were worth sharing.

“What if my idea is not good enough?”

“What if I say something wrong?”

LOOKING BACK

I was learning technical skills, but the bigger challenge was learning to trust my own perspective.

02
TURNING POINT

The First Time I Chose to Speak Anyway

During one planning session, I had an idea that could improve how we approached community engagement. My first instinct was the same as before: stay quiet.

But this time, I spoke.

THE MOMENT

My idea did not need to be perfect to be valuable.

The team listened. They asked questions. We refined the idea together, and eventually it became something the team could actually use.

WHAT CHANGED

Confidence is not certainty. Sometimes it is simply the willingness to contribute before you feel completely ready.

03
BEYOND TECHNICAL SKILLS

The Skills No Tutorial Could Fully Teach Me

Working on real community initiatives exposed me to challenges that could not be solved by technical knowledge alone. I had to communicate, coordinate, make decisions, and work with people who had different perspectives.

Communication

Learning how to explain an idea clearly, receive feedback, and speak with more confidence.

Collaboration

Working across teams with different responsibilities, priorities, personalities, and working styles.

Strategic Thinking

Moving beyond “let's post something” toward campaigns, objectives, audiences, timing, and measurable outcomes.

Ownership

Understanding that leadership often begins by taking responsibility before someone explicitly asks you to.

04
THE JOURNEY

From Quiet Volunteer to Marketing Co-Lead

Growth did not happen in one dramatic moment. It happened gradually through repeated opportunities to contribute, make mistakes, improve, and take responsibility.

01
STUDENT VOLUNTEER

Observe & Learn

Learning how the community works, supporting initiatives, and absorbing everything I could.

02
ACTIVE CONTRIBUTOR

Speak & Participate

Sharing ideas, contributing to planning, and becoming more comfortable expressing my perspective.

03
RESPONSIBILITY

Own & Execute

Taking responsibility for campaigns, deliverables, coordination, and results.

04
MARKETING CO-LEAD

Guide & Lead

Helping shape strategy, guide initiatives, coordinate teams, and contribute to organizational direction.

LEADERSHIP REALIZATION

Leadership is not about being the loudest person in the room.

It is about knowing when your voice can help, taking responsibility for what follows, and creating space for others to contribute too.

05
PROFESSIONAL IMPACT

Volunteer Experience Became Real-World Experience

Python Philippines gave me opportunities to practice skills that later became directly relevant to professional work.

01

Technical ability becomes more valuable when you can communicate ideas clearly.

02

Community work can provide real experience in deadlines, stakeholders, teamwork, and accountability.

03

Confidence is developed through repeated action, not by waiting until fear disappears.

04

Leadership is built through consistency, reliability, and trust.

THE BIGGER LESSON

Career growth does not happen only inside a job.

Communities, volunteer organizations, and side projects can become powerful environments for developing professional judgment, leadership, confidence, and practical experience.

TO STUDENTS & EARLY-CAREER PROFESSIONALS

Do Not Wait Until You Feel Ready.

01

Join communities where you can learn from people outside your usual environment.

02

Volunteer for responsibilities that make you slightly uncomfortable.

03

Share ideas even when they are not perfectly formed.

04

Treat volunteer responsibilities with the same professionalism you would bring to paid work.

05

Measure your growth not only by titles, but by what you are now capable of doing that once scared you.

THE BOTTOM LINE

Volunteerism Did Not Distract Me From My Career. It Accelerated It.

My journey with Python Philippines is more than a line on my résumé. It represents the transition from self-doubt to participation, from participation to responsibility, and from responsibility to leadership.

I joined because I wanted to learn more about technology. I stayed because I discovered something equally important: growth happens when you give yourself opportunities to contribute before you feel completely ready.
